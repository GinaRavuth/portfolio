IT Capstone Final Project: Ordino

For my Capstone project, my group and I developed a hardware inventory system for the IT department. This inventory system is focused on one room, 2208, in the Information Technology building. The system includes all the hardware in room 2208 such as TVs, monitors, workstations, routers and much more. Students will be able to see the status, description, type and location of items. In this case, a checkout process will be used to checkout and return hardware items. We called this system Ordino, which is latin for "order".


My roles include Front-End Developer, Designer, and Project Manager for Ordino. As Front-End Developer, I worked with HTML/HTML5 + CSS and the Bootstrap framework to develop the front end aspect of the system. As Designer, I wireframed how the system was going to look and tested out colors to see what would fit. Last, as Project Manager, I made sure our group had everything together, reminded us of deadlines, and made sure we were on task.

Rural Sourcing Inc. Hackathon Competition

Rural Sourcing, Inc. (RSI) is the leader in domestic sourcing, a cost-effective alternative to offshoring for technology development and the ongoing support and maintenance for critical business applications (as said on company overview on facebook). RSI is located in Augusta, GA and holds a hackathon competition every year in October. The competition requires a group of students or individual to develop a website or system for RSI's company of choice within a 4 hour time period. RSI provides all content for the competition. The rest is up to the groups to develop. Universities from all over South Georgia were able to compete.


My group included me, my friend Travis Crowe and my sister Renita Ravuth. Travis and I had the role of front-end developers while Renita was designer. We managed to finish five pages within the four hour time period. Let's just say those four hours were intense. We focused on making sure our site functioned properly and the design was intriguing. We used HTML/CSS and the Bootstrap framework. At the end of the four hours, each group was to present their developed site. My group placed 3rd place out of 12 and were one of Georgia Southern's first group to place.
